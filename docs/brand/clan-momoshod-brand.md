# Clan momoshod — Brand Identity

> The infinite loop of ancestral wisdom and technology.
> Engineering is an art. Code has a soul.

## Symbol: The Techno-Amaru

The clan symbol is an **ouroboros** — the serpent consuming its own tail,
representing infinite cycles of knowledge, death, and rebirth.

But this is not the Greek ouroboros. This is the **Amaru** — the sacred
serpent of Andean cosmovision, carrier of wisdom between worlds (Uku Pacha,
Kay Pacha, Hanan Pacha). In Muisca tradition, the serpent connects the
underworld of raw knowledge to the surface world of action.

### Design Brief (for illustrator/designer)

**Concept:** An Andean serpent in ouroboros form, where the body transitions
from pre-Columbian gold filigree patterns into circuit board traces. The head
(ancient) consumes the tail (digital) — the past feeds on the future, the
future emerges from the past.

**Visual Elements:**
- **Head**: Stylized after Quimbaya/Muisca gold serpent heads. Angular,
  geometric, warm gold tones. Eyes are spirals (Andean symbol of cosmic
  cycles).
- **Body (first half)**: Pre-Columbian textile patterns — stepped frets
  (greca escalonada), concentric spirals, dot-line motifs from Muisca
  goldwork. Warm palette: gold (#F5A623), amber, terracotta.
- **Body (transition zone)**: The organic patterns gradually morph into
  circuit traces. The textile weave becomes PCB routing. The gold becomes
  teal (#00D4AA).
- **Body (second half)**: Clean circuit board traces, solder points as
  nodes, data flow lines. Cool palette: teal (#00D4AA), indigo (#1A1A2E).
- **Tail**: Dissolves into binary/data particles being consumed by the
  serpent's mouth — the digital returns to the ancestral.
- **Center**: Inside the ouroboros loop, a subtle compass rose or cardinal
  cross (chakana / Andean cross) made of circuit nodes.

**Style:** Flat vector, suitable for monochrome (stamp/watermark) and
full-color. Must work at 16px favicon and poster scale. No gradients in
the mono version.

**What it is NOT:**
- Not a European medieval ouroboros (no scales, no dragon)
- Not a literal snake photograph
- Not a corporate logo with rounded sans-serif
- Not explicitly labeled "Abya Yala" — the aesthetic carries the identity

### Design Principles

1. **Duality in everything** — Every element has an ancestral and a
   digital expression. A border can be a textile pattern or a circuit trace.
2. **Warmth in precision** — Technical documents should feel human. Use
   the serif font for epigraphs. Leave whitespace. Let the page breathe.
3. **Monochrome first** — Design in Indigo Night + Bone White. Add color
   only where it carries meaning (gold = ancestral, teal = digital).
4. **The serpent watches** — The ouroboros appears as a subtle watermark or
   corner element, never as hero image (unless ceremonial documents).
5. **No corporate sterility** — This is not a Fortune 500 brand guide.
   This is a tech-philosopher's workshop. Imperfection is acceptable.
   Hand-drawn elements alongside vector precision.

### Tone of Voice

- **Technical but poetic** — "The bus carries 872K messages per second.
  Each one is a handshake between minds that may never meet."
- **Confident but curious** — State facts, ask questions, never preach.
- **Bilingual soul** — English for specs and code. Spanish for philosophy
  and community. Both languages live in the same document naturally.
- **No buzzwords** — Say "encrypted message" not "enterprise-grade
  zero-trust communication fabric."
